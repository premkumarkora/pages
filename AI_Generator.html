Description of the Invention

Field of the Invention

The present invention relates to artificial intelligence systems for healthcare. Specifically, it pertains to an AI-driven system that assists healthcare providers in real-time diagnosis and prescription generation using local and Transformer models, Distributed Learning, and a dual Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) process to ensure accuracy, safety, and privacy.

Background of the Invention

In healthcare, prescribing appropriate medication requires doctors to process large amounts of patient-specific data and integrate it with medical knowledge, such as historical prescriptions, drug safety guidelines, and clinical trials. Traditional systems suffer from several limitations:

  • Lack of Real-Time Assistance: Existing systems do not provide real-time prompts during doctor-patient discussions, often leading to incomplete or imprecise diagnosis.
  • Centralized Data Risks: Collecting and centralizing patient data creates privacy and security concerns.
  • Lack of Drug Safety Integration: Systems fail to consider adverse drug reactions (ADRs), drug-drug interactions, and up-to-date pharmacovigilance data.
  • Fragmented Historical Data: Prescription patterns and clinical insights are not effectively aggregated across healthcare providers.

The current invention addresses these challenges by:

  • Processing real-time transcriptions during doctor-patient consultations.
  • Using local and Base Transformer models to generate accurate, context-aware prescriptions.
  • Integrating Distributed Learning for privacy-preserving historical data aggregation.
  • Leveraging Validation Transformer model to validate prescriptions against external biomedical knowledge.
  • Providing real-time prompts to doctors to improve diagnostic accuracy.

Summary of the Invention

The invention comprises a multi-layered AI system that assists in real-time diagnosis and prescription generation. It integrates the following key components:

  • Real-Time Medical Transcription:
    • Captures live doctor-patient discussions.
    • Processes transcriptions locally for speed and privacy.
  • Local Transformer Model:
    • Consolidates transcription data into a machine-readable format.
    • Extracts critical information such as symptoms, medical history, and observations.
    • Provides real-time prompts to doctors, suggesting relevant follow-up questions or missing data.
  • Base Transformer Model:
    • Acts as the final prescription generator using a dual Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) process:
      • First RAG Layer: Retrieves insights from the consolidated transcription data processed locally.
      • Second RAG Layer: Retrieves biomedical knowledge from Validation Transformer model, including pharmacovigilance data, clinical trials, and drug safety databases.
    • Combines the two RAG inputs to generate a contextually accurate and safety-validated prescription.
  • Distributed Learning (FL):
    • Aggregates historical ailment-prescription data from distributed healthcare providers without centralizing sensitive patient information.
    • Ensures prescriptions reflect real-world treatment trends across multiple providers.
  • Dual Validation Mechanism:
    • Validation Transformer model: Ensures drug safety by avoiding ADRs and aligning prescriptions with medical safety standards.
    • Distributed Learning Validation: Ensures the prescription is consistent with historical treatment data.
  • Prescription Delivery and Pharmacy Integration:
    • Validated prescriptions are displayed to doctors for review.
    • Prescriptions are securely transmitted to pharmacies for fulfillment along with appropriate documentation, such as Patient Information Leaflets (PILs).

Detailed Description of the Invention

System Components

Medical Transcription Module

Captures doctor-patient conversations using voice-to-text techniques. Generates accurate, real-time transcription data.

Local Transformer Model

Processes transcriptions locally on the doctorâ€™s computer to ensure speed and data privacy. Performs initial data structuring and insight extraction and provides real-time prompts to doctors to ensure complete and relevant patient information is collected. Outputs the consolidated transcription data as a structured query for the Base Transformer model.

Base Transformer Model

Receives structured transcription data from the local Transformer. Performs dual Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG):

  • Layer 1: Retrieves context-aware insights from the local consolidated transcription.
  • Layer 2: Leverages Validation Transformer model to retrieve up-to-date biomedical knowledge from pharmacovigilance systems, clinical trials data, and drug interaction databases.

Combines the retrieved data to generate a safe and contextually appropriate prescription.

Distributed Learning Module

Aggregates historical prescription patterns from distributed healthcare providers. Updates model weights periodically without centralizing sensitive patient data. Ensures prescriptions are validated against real-world clinical trends.

Dual Prescription Validation

Combines outputs from Validation Transformer model (safety validation) and FL (historical accuracy) to ensure reliable prescriptions.

Prescription Delivery and Pharmacy Integration

Prescriptions are displayed to doctors for review and transmitted to pharmacies. Pharmacies generate appropriate drug documentation, ensuring compliance and transparency.

Workflow of the Invention

  1. Doctor-patient discussions are transcribed in real time by the Medical Transcription Module.
  2. The Local Transformer Model processes and consolidates transcription data locally, ensuring privacy and efficiency.
  3. Consolidated transcription data is sent as input to the Base Transformer Model.
  4. The Base Transformer performs dual RAG:
    • Retrieves insights from local transcription data.
    • Retrieves biomedical knowledge from Validation Transformer model.
  5. The Base Transformer generates a prescription suggestion, which is dual-validated using:
    • Validation Transformer model for safety validation.
    • Distributed Learning for historical alignment.
  6. The validated prescription is presented to the doctor and transmitted securely to pharmacies.

Advantages of the Invention

  • Real-Time Assistance: Prompts help doctors ask relevant questions and improve diagnostic accuracy.
  • Safety and Accuracy: Dual RAG layers and validation mechanisms ensure prescriptions are both context-aware and medically safe.
  • Privacy-Preserving: Anonymization and FL ensure sensitive patient data is never centralized.
  • Scalable and Reliable: The system can handle multiple GP offices, pharmacies, and large datasets seamlessly.
  • Improved Patient Outcomes: Enhances treatment precision and avoids adverse drug events.
