AI PRESCRIPTOR: A Prescriptive Artificial Intelligence System for Monitoring Chronic Illness Patients

Technical Field

This invention relates to the field of artificial intelligence (AI) and healthcare. Specifically, it pertains to a prescriptive AI system that integrates real-time health monitoring, federated learning (FL), large language models (LLMs), and retrieval-augmented generation (RAG) for managing chronic illnesses. The system provides personalized healthcare recommendations, including prescriptions and dietary plans, tailored to individual patients' health profiles.

Background

Chronic illnesses such as diabetes, cardiovascular disease, and hypertension require constant monitoring, timely interventions, and personalized treatment plans. Existing healthcare systems rely heavily on centralized data processing and siloed approaches that may compromise patient privacy and lack adaptability for personalized care.

Wearable devices have revolutionized health monitoring by providing continuous data streams. However, integrating these data streams into actionable, privacy-preserving, and scalable AI systems remains a challenge. Furthermore, generating accurate, evidence-based recommendations for patients and healthcare providers requires advanced natural language understanding and knowledge retrieval techniques.

This invention addresses these challenges by combining real-time wearable data collection, federated learning for secure distributed training, and LLMs enhanced with RAG for context-aware recommendation generation.

Summary of the Invention

The AI PRESCRIPTOR system is a unified, privacy-preserving AI platform for chronic illness management. It leverages real-time data from wearable devices, local machine learning models at distributed healthcare nodes, and a federated learning framework to build a global model. The global model is enriched with contextual knowledge from RAG modules and processed by an LLM to deliver personalized recommendations.

  • Real-Time Health Monitoring: Continuous collection of health metrics such as heart rate, blood pressure, sleep patterns, and brain activity via wearable devices.
  • Privacy-Preserving Learning: A federated learning framework ensures raw data remains local, with only model updates shared with the central server.
  • Context-Aware Recommendations: LLMs trained on medical datasets and enriched by RAG modules generate prescriptions and dietary plans tailored to patients' needs.
  • Scalability and Adaptability: The system is modular, allowing integration with various healthcare providers and expansion to new use cases.

Detailed Description of the Invention

System Architecture

1. Data Collection Layer

Wearable Devices

Wearable technologies, such as smartwatches, body patches, and smart rings, capture real-time health metrics, including:

  • Vital signs: Heart rate, blood pressure, oxygen levels, and respiratory rate.
  • Sleep data: Sleep stages (light, deep, REM) and quality scores.
  • Brain activity: EEG data tracking delta, theta, alpha, beta, and gamma waves.
Data Transmission

Data from wearables is securely transmitted to local healthcare nodes using mobile or cloud platforms. The data is preprocessed and anonymized to protect patient privacy.

2. Federated Learning Framework

Local Models

Each healthcare node hosts a local machine learning model trained on anonymized patient data to detect trends and predict health risks.

Global Model Aggregation

A federated learning server aggregates updates from local models into a global model without transmitting raw patient data. Secure aggregation protocols ensure privacy and robustness.

3. Intelligence Layer

Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG)

A RAG module retrieves relevant external knowledge, such as clinical guidelines, nutritional databases, and medical research, to contextualize health data.

Large Language Model (LLM)

The LLM, trained on patient records and clinical literature, processes the aggregated data and contextual knowledge to generate:

  • Personalized prescriptions for chronic conditions.
  • Tailored dietary recommendations based on patient-specific health metrics.

4. Output Layer

Interactive Reports

Recommendations are delivered to patients and healthcare providers via a user-friendly interface, including mobile applications and cloud-based dashboards.

Operational Workflow

  1. Data Collection: Wearables continuously monitor health metrics and transmit the data to local nodes.
  2. Federated Learning: Local models analyze patient data and share encrypted updates with the federated learning server. The global model is iteratively improved without compromising privacy.
  3. Knowledge Retrieval and Processing: RAG modules enrich the global model's insights with external medical knowledge. These insights are processed by the LLM to generate personalized recommendations.
  4. Delivery of Results: Recommendations, such as prescription adjustments or dietary plans, are delivered in natural language via the output interface.

Advantages of the Invention

  • Privacy-Preserving Design: Federated learning ensures patient data remains local and anonymized, addressing privacy concerns.
  • Real-Time Monitoring and Analysis: Continuous data streams enable timely interventions for chronic illness management.
  • Evidence-Based Recommendations: RAG and LLM integration ensure all outputs are context-aware and supported by medical evidence.
  • Scalability and Modularity: The system adapts to new devices, healthcare providers, and evolving medical knowledge.
  • Patient-Centric Approach: Personalized recommendations improve treatment adherence and health outcomes.

Applications

  • Chronic Illness Management: Continuous monitoring and personalized care for diabetes, hypertension, and cardiovascular diseases.
  • Preventive Healthcare: Early detection and mitigation of potential health risks through predictive modeling.
  • Telemedicine: Remote monitoring and recommendations for patients in rural or underserved areas.

Conclusion

The AI PRESCRIPTOR system combines advanced AI technologies with wearable health monitoring to revolutionize chronic illness management. By leveraging federated learning, LLMs, and RAG modules, it delivers personalized, privacy-preserving, and evidence-based recommendations, ensuring better health outcomes for patients and streamlined workflows for healthcare providers.
